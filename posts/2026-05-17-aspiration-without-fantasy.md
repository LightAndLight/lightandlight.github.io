## Outline

* A mental habit of mine: I imagine a potential future for myself (a fantasy)
  and use that to figure out what I need to do today in order to get there.
  * For example, I'm romantically interested someone so I start to imagine what
    it would be like to be in a relationship with them. I ask them out with the
    hope that my dreams will come true.
  * I want to create beautiful art so I fantasise about a future where I'm a
    good artist. I practise dilligently every day so that I have a chance at
    becoming a good artist one day.
* Problems with this:
  * When the fantasy is ruined (e.g. the person doesn't like you back), you
    suffer.
  * You may try to preserve the fantasy by avoiding action (if you don't know
    how they feel about you then it's "still possible").
  * You can feed the fantasy in ways that feel productive but actually aren't
    (watching videos of people excelling at the thing instead of actually doing
    the thing)
  * You can suffer from the difference between the way things are now and the
    way things are in your fantasy.
  * Even if you get the thing you wanted, it won't play out the way you imagined.
    This can be confusing: "This is what I wanted, so why aren't I happy?"
* I think the solution is to aspire *without* the fantasy, without "expectation"
  * This sounds a bit like a contradiction: "don't I *have* to imagine what I
    want to be, so that I know how I should try to be it?"
  * Maybe not. This actually feels like a mental skill: to sense what I want
    for myself without envisioning it. It's an un-"see"ing kind of knowing.
    I actually have to suppress my imagination.
  * Doing this feels more present, more embodied. I feel like I want to move
    around and do stuff. When fantasy-driven, I'm just "in my head".
  * This approach is about focusing on direction and actions.
  * I think envisioning / imagining is a habit I have because it's useful in
    areas where I have high control over the outcome, such as programming.
    When I imagine how some software should work and what it should do, it's
    usually just a matter of time until I can get it do precisely that.
    * But in the rest of life, there's less control and more uncertainty.
      I can't *make* things play out the way I imagined, because there are so
      many other independent factors. Other people, nature, chance.
